Weather Forecast: A storm system tracking by to our north will push a pair of fronts through our region, producing periods of rain this this morning. The rain will give way to more localized lake effect rain and snow showers east of the lakes by midday and this afternoon, continuing through Wednesday. While a few inches of wet snow will be possible in a few high elevation locations, there should not be any widespread travel concerns. There will be the chance for mixed rain and wet snow regionwide for Thanksgiving Day, but relatively mild temperatures should keep travel impacts minor. Much colder air will then pour into the Great Lakes Friday through the weekend, setting the stage for SIGNIFICANT lake effect snow east of Lake Erie and Lake Ontario.
