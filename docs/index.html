Weather Forecast: A wedge of high pressure extending south from Quebec will maintain fair dry weather across the vast majority of our region today, but a warm front lurking over northeast Ohio could support a shower or two for parts of the Southern Tier late today and this evening. Conditions will then significantly deteriorate later Wednesday through the end of the week, as a large complex storm system will slowly push across the Great Lakes to New England. This will result in a soaking rain Wednesday night, then a prolonged period of miserable weather into the weekend that will include a mix of rain and wet snow. The first accumulating snow of the season will be possible, especially across the higher terrain.
