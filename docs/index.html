Weather Forecast: Low pressure will pass by just north of the Great Lakes later today through Thursday. This system will bring periods of snow and possibly a few snow squalls, followed by lake effect snow east and southeast of the lakes lasting through at least late Thursday night. Winds will also be quite strong, with considerable blowing and drifting snow and difficult travel conditions, especially late tonight through Thursday night. The greatest snow amounts will be found across the higher terrain east of Lake Erie and Lake Ontario, but all areas will receive at least some accumulation.
